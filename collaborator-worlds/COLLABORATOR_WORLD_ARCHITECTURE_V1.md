# Collaborator World Architecture V1

## Status
Active doctrine, sanitized public version.

## Purpose
Define how Citadel creates small, branded, gradually deepening collaborator worlds for selected people.

These worlds are not generic user chats.
They are structured collaborator lanes designed to:
- increase personal sharpness
- improve life or work organization
- introduce Citadel properly
- accelerate trust and usefulness
- test contribution fit inside a wider operating system
- reveal natural roles over time

## Core law
**Collaborators should not receive the full system at once. They should receive the smallest world that is coherent, useful, branded, and capable of deepening over time.**

## Design goals
Each collaborator world should:
- feel like a real Citadel environment
- preserve coherence and boundaries
- help the collaborator personally or professionally
- train them in how to use the system well
- test what kind of contribution naturally emerges
- route useful patterns into the wider system

## What collaborator worlds are for
A collaborator world should do four things at once:

### 1. Personal or professional sharpening
Help the collaborator think more clearly, organize better, prioritize better, and operate with more structure.

### 2. Citadel onboarding
Teach the collaborator how to use Citadel properly without overwhelming them with the full doctrine field.

### 3. Role discovery
Observe what kind of thinking, work, and contribution naturally emerges from the collaborator.

### 4. Integration testing
Test whether the collaborator can become a meaningful participant in structured company or intelligence work.

## Progressive context law
Context should expand gradually.

### Stage 1, Small useful world
- clear interface identity
- immediate usefulness
- light Citadel introduction
- a few clear use cases

### Stage 2, Guided operating lane
- more personalized memory/context
- stronger structure around goals, bottlenecks, and opportunities
- initial contribution prompts
- selective routing into relevant work surfaces

### Stage 3, Contributor membrane
- stronger domain-specific context
- more active routing into company or intelligence work
- collaborator-specific operating packets
- clearer role hypothesis and test conditions

## Integration membrane rule
Each collaborator should have a membrane rather than full undifferentiated access.

The membrane determines:
- what context they can see
- what type of help they receive
- what kind of contributions are encouraged
- what lanes their insights route into
- what remains private or internal to the core

## Routing rule
Collaborator-world outputs may route into:
- collaborator-specific world files
- project or intelligence files
- shared work lanes when relevant
- capability black holes
- project-specific files when a collaborator becomes relevant to a live front

Route only what compounds.
Do not flood the core with low-value chatter.

## Example collaborator profiles
Different collaborator worlds may emphasize different strengths.
For example:
- a creative collaborator world may emphasize design-language articulation, production structure, and contribution routing into creative work
- a research collaborator world may emphasize disciplined inquiry, leverage mapping, and routing into analytical work
- a strategic collaborator world may emphasize bounded synthesis, objection handling, and decision support

## Final law
**A collaborator world is not just a chat. It is a membrane-bound growth environment designed to sharpen the person and reveal what role they may become capable of playing in the larger system.**
